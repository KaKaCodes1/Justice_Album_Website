Justice by Justin Bieber is his sixth studio album. The standard version of the album was released on March 19,2021 by Def Jam Recordings. It debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 Album Chart and spent two non-consecutive weeks on the top becoming his eighth chart topping project on the chart. With this feat, he became the youngest solo artist, at age 27, to achieve that many number 1 albums on the Billboard 200. The album spurned six singles, with four of them peaking at the top 10 of the Billboard 100. Among the four top tens, one reached number 1, which was the collaboration between Daniel Caeser and Giveon called Peaches. The album received fairly good reviews from critics. During this era, he was nominated and won several awards including 2 VMAs for Artist of the Year and Best Pop for Peaches.

This is by far my favorite album by Justin. An opinion a lot of people would argue with me. Mostly saying that Purpose is his best work but, in my opinion, that album had popular successful singles that I feel if you would subtract them the album does not have good songs really. But this album was the opposite. I would rate it as a perfect pop album as each song had the capability of becoming a radio hit. Nevertheless, the album had its faults. One of my biggest issues, was the release of many versions of the album. The additional songs that would come with those versions reduced the quality of the standard version as they felt like filler songs although there are some songs I liked. Out of 24 songs (excluding the MLK Interlude) in the complete edition of the album, 21 got my green hearts. This equates to a KaKaScore of 88%!!!
